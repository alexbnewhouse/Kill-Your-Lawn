Common Objections
"It'll look messy."
Design and intention make the difference. Clean edges, defined paths, and a simple sign signal that this is deliberate. It looks sparse in year one and beautiful by year three.
"Won't it attract pests?"
Lawns attract pests — grubs bring skunks and raccoons. Mosquitoes breed in standing water, not plants. Natives support predatory insects that keep pest populations down.
"My HOA won't allow it."
Colorado HB 21-1229 (2021) and SB 23-178 (2023) prohibit HOAs from banning native or drought-tolerant landscaping. The law is on your side.
"Is it a fire hazard?"
Well-designed native gardens — with rock mulch and low-growing prairie species — are often more fire-resistant than a dried-out July lawn.
"Won't it lower my property value?"
Water savings are a real selling point in the West. Studies in arid climates show xeriscape landscaping is neutral to positive on property values.
"This seems like a lot of work."
Lawns require constant maintenance. A native garden is front-loaded — hard in year one, easier each year after. By year three, you're mostly watching.
How to Kill Your Lawn

Five methods, in order of effort

  • Sheet mulching — cardboard + 4–6" wood chips. Year-round. Builds soil as it smothers.
  • Solarization — clear plastic in summer heat for 6–8 weeks. Free and effective.
  • Tarp smothering — black poly tarps for 6–12 months. Reusable, low effort.
  • Sod cutting — rent a machine, strip it out, plant immediately.
  • Native lawn swap — replace bluegrass with blue grama + buffalo grass. Still a lawn, just the right one.
Start Small

You don't have to do it all at once

  • The hell strip — most visible, least precious, easiest win
  • One corner bed — define it with clean edges, plant 5–10 species
  • A pollinator patch — 6×6 ft of coneflower, bergamot, prairie clover
  • Lawn on a diet — convert 25% per year over four years

Colorado Law

HB 21-1229 & SB 23-178

HOAs cannot prohibit drought-tolerant or native plant landscaping. They must offer pre-approved water-wise designs. If your HOA fines you, cite SB 23-178.
